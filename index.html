Proposed Title: Artificial Intelligence–Driven Movement Disorder Detection from Videos and Brain MRI images:A Pilot Study in Bangladesh

Project Overview

The objective of this sub-project is to develop and validate a multimodal deep learning-based system for the early detection and severity assessment of Autism Spectrum Disorder (ASD) and Parkinson’s Disease (PD) in Bangladeshi patients. The proposed system will integrate movement videos and brain MRI data to deliver an affordable, accurate, and clinically reliable diagnostic solution.

By facilitating early diagnosis and timely intervention, the system aims to improve patient outcomes and quality of life. Furthermore, it is designed to be scalable and deployable across hospitals and healthcare facilities throughout Bangladesh, supporting rural and underserved communities where access to specialized services is limited.

Specific Objectives

A

Data Collection & Alignment

Creation of a subject-aligned multimodal dataset from Bangladeshi patients diagnosed with PD, ASD, and healthy controls. Reflects local demographic and clinical conditions, ensuring hyper-relevance to Bangladesh’s unique healthcare landscape.

B

Feature Extraction

Capturing critical kinematic characteristics including distances and angles between skeletal markers. Introducing advanced computational parameters like Thumb-to-Index Vector Velocity and Acceleration to isolate 40 distinct motor-control markers.

C

Model Development

Leveraging state-of-the-art deep learning models to process sequential spatial-temporal relationships within video frames alongside structural anomalies from complex brain MRI imaging blocks.

D

Validation & Clinical Integration

Rigorous multicenter evaluations prioritizing balanced precision, recall rates, and F1-scores to ensure system resilience against varied equipment manufacturers, processing arrays, and specific scanner models.

E

Deployment & Capacity Building

Engineering responsive native cross-platform desktop and mobile app deployment profiles alongside practical, structured workshops pushing for sustainable clinical assimilation across internal regional networks.

Project Motivations

01

Healthcare Need & Tech Opportunity

ASD and PD remain heavily underdiagnosed in regional centers due to cost barriers and limited specialist access. Combining streamlined consumer-grade video metrics with critical structural neural telemetry answers this national dilemma cleanly.

02

Local Relevance & Inclusion

Building intelligence layers natively using demographic groups within the delta helps establish clinical validity. We maintain a strict focus on gender parity, committing to a minimum of 50% female inclusion profiles across all advanced technical roles and research paths.

03

Long-Term Structural Vision

Our final mission aims past laboratory walls, deploying high-efficiency, edge-ready, scalable diagnostic systems into distant upazila levels, bridging gaps for isolated and underserved populations.

SWOT Analysis

Strengths

First-of-its-kind multimodal structural biomarker framework optimized natively for local deployment configurations; cost-efficient pipeline architecture designed explicitly around low-resource operational realities.

Weaknesses

Initial training volumes remain limited, which impacts broad generalized output models; high dependencies on quality primary MRI inputs; demands intricate coordination across distinct clinical fields.

Opportunities

Tremendous capacity for widespread domestic institutional inclusion; provides immediate technological training paths for local engineering pipelines; builds domestic health-tech ownership.

Threats

Strict data privacy compliance parameters; extreme regional variances across hospital imaging equipment pools; localized user adoption hesitations in clinical workflows.
